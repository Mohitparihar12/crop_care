← Fertilize your nursery

2 weeks before seedling


• Apply the suggested amount of nitrogen fertilizer after 10 days of seedling emergence.
• The recommended amount of fertilizer is 16 kg of DAP per acre or 6.5 kg of urea per acre + 46 kg of super phosphate per acre.
• Apply as a top dressing into your nursery.

← Fertilization before transplanting

1 week before seedling


• Broadcast 10% of your available nitrogen evenly on your field, all of your phosphorus and potassium, and incorporate the fertilizers just before sowing or transplanting as a basal dose slightly into the soil.
• Apply 6 kg of per acre of urea, 21 kg per acre of DAP and 23 kg per acre of MOP.
• Do not use chemical fertilizer if you need more than a 5 kg paddy to pay for 1 kg of fertilizer. Fertilizers must be stored in a dry, cool place that is out of children's reach.
• Do not apply high rates of fertilizer for traditional varieties, as they may have limited response and cause lodging.

Caution: Excessive use of nitrogenous fertilizers, particularly during flowering, causes sterility. and consequently, a significant reduction in yield.

← Fertilization at tillering stage

Week 4


• Apply one-third of the nitrogen as a top dress during the early tillering stage.
• If you are using urea, then apply 25 kg per acre.
• Apply less nitrogen if you are planting inbred seeds.
• Drain out water before topdressing with nitrogen fertilizer, and let in water 24 hours later.
• Apply the fertilizer by broadcasting as evenly as possible.
• Do not apply fertilizers before the rain.
• In established crops, apply chemical fertilizer only in standing water and evenly across the whole field.
• Do not apply high rates of fertilizer for traditional varieties, as they may have limited response and cause lodging.

← Fertilization at the panicle initiation stage

Week 9


• Apply the rest of the nitrogen dose a week before panicle initiation.
• If you are using urea, apply 25 kg per acre.
