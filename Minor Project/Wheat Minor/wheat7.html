Irrigation of wheat

Week 1

Here are some recommendations on irrigation:
• Water storage in the root zone can be achieved by controlling runoff, improving infiltration and adopting different water harvesting methods.
• To conserve more water, the soil can be loosened by tillage up to a depth of more than 20cm using a chisel plough before the advent of the rainy season to enhance the water permeation capacity of the soil.
• Irrigate at times of planting, late tillering, late jointing, flowering, milk and dough stages.
• Irrigate a few more times if the soil is very light or sandy.

A recommended schedule for irrigation is as follows:
• First at planting.
• Second at 20-25 days after sowing.
• Third at 35-42 days after sowing.
• Fourth at 70-84 days after sowing.
• Fifth at 90-105 days after sowing.
