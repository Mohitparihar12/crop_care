Appropriate storage conditions

week 18

Ideally, your harvest is stored in a place that meets the following criteria:
• Protection from insects, rodents, and birds.
• Good hygienic conditions.
• Easy loading and unloading.
• Efficient use of space.
• Easy maintenance and management.
• Prevention of moisture re-entering the grain after drying.

Control pests in storage

week 18

• A small number of resident insects in a bin, or introduced with the grain, can lead to a serious infestation if the grain is warm, or if the grain remains in storage for a long time.
• Clean the storage area well before storing a new grain.
Follow customary pest control techniques, such as:
• Controlled climate, including cooling and aeration to reduce the insect reproductive rate and humidity.
• Controlled and airtight storage atmospheres.
• Drying grain in summer in sunlight to reduce the moisture content.
• Heat treatment (more than 60° C) to disinfect grain.
• Physical barriers to protect uninfested grain.
• Fumigation with penetrating gases.
• Contact insecticides to control flying insects.
• Adding an insecticide or grain protectant.
• Trapping to control rodents.
