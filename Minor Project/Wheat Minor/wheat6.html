Applying post-emergence herbicides

Week 3

In addition to the many cultural weeding methods, chemical methods against weeds in wheat can be applied. Weeds of wheat can be controlled with the use of post-emergence herbicides. Most of the weeds in wheat are from the broad-leaf family.
Isoproturon (product) can be applied 2-3 days before the first irrigation takes place:
• On heavy-texture soils, use Isoproturon 75 WP at 500 g per acre.
•medium-textured soils, use Isoproturon 75 WP at 400 g per acre.
• Spray the herbicide by dissolving it in 200 litres of water with a knapsack sprayer fitted with a flat fan, or use a tractor-mounted sprayer.
•Spray patiently, and as uniformly as possible.
Irrigate lightly within 2-3 days of spraying the herbicide.
To control broad-leaf weeds, apply 2,4-D dimethylamine salt 58 EC 0.2 to 0.3 kg per acre 35-45 days after sowing wheat on time, or 45-55 days after sowing wheat late.
• Please take care: If gram, sarson or any other broad-leaf crop is sown in wheat, do not apply 2,4-D at all.
• If you have any doubts, please contact your local agricultural extension officer.
Please review the safety recommendations below before applying any herbicide:
• Always mix clean water with chemicals. Refrain from using muddy water, as this reduces the chemicals' efficacy.
• Ensure that all equipment, such as spray tanks, booms, and nozzles, are well-cleaned after every use.
• Do not mix different chemicals together unless recommended.
• Apply chemicals at the recommended rate.
• Remember to wear proper protective equipment (i.e., gloves, breathing mask, goggles, and protective clothing) when.
• Mixing the product is the time of greatest concern, as the product is in its most concentrated form. When mixing the product, always stand upwind and wear protective clothing - especially face protection.
• Wash contaminated clothes separately from other household cloths.
• Clean blocked nozzles with a non-abrasive implement. Never clean nozzles by blowing on them with the mouth.
• Discard the containers properly.

Manual or mechanical weeding

Week 3

• Perform the first hoeing before the first irrigation.
• Perform the second hoeing after the first irrigation e.g. with a wheel hoe.
• The types of weeds present in different areas of the wheat crop vary depending upon soil type and climate.
There are several measures that may minimize weed growth:
• The infestation gullidanda/sitti (phalaris minor) in wheat can be reduced by early sowing (last week of October/1st week of November) and by rotating wheat with berseem, a potato-based cropping system, raya, gob sarson, winter maize, etc.
• Growth and development of gullidanda can also be suppressed by sowing wheat at 15 cm spacing, or by selecting quick-growing wheat varieties like PBW 502, PBW 343 and WH 542.
• Preparation of soil mulch after seedbed preparation also helps in eliminating the first flush of Gullidanda.
Caution: Don't use a spade for weeding, as it may cause soil erosion.
