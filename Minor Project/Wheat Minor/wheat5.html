Prepare the field

2 Weeks before seeding

In a Nutshell
To avoid quick loss of moisture and get uniform germination of seeds and better root growth, prepare your field properly.
Wheat is often planted after the harvest of the kharif crop. To prepare your field, follow these steps:
step-1
• If soil is too dry, apply one pre-sowing irrigation to improve moisture content in your soil.
step-2
• Harrow the soil 2 times to prepare the soil loose.
step-3
• Apply well-decomposed farmyard manure well into the soil.
• Harrow the soil for a third and last time.
step-4
• Level the soil with a planker.
step-5
• Plough the remaining straw of your kharif crop (probably rice straw) up to 15-20 cm deep into the soil. Mixing of straw improves soil properties over a long period. For proper incorporation of straw, the land should be disc-harrowed twice at least two weeks before planting wheat.
step-6
• If the soil is heavy conduct one more ploughing before leveling the soil.
