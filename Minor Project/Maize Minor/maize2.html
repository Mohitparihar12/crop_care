Apply the right seed rate

1 Weeks before seedling

To achieve higher productivity and resource-use efficiency, optimum plant stand is the key factor. The seed rate varies depending on the purpose, seed size, plant type, season, sowing methods, etc. Here are some recommendations based on grain type:
• Normal grain 20 kg per ha (8 kg per acre), spacing should be 60x20cm.
• Sweet corn 8 kg per ha (3 kg per acre), spacing should be 75x25cm.
• Fodder maize 50 kg per ha (20 kg per acre), spacing should be 30x10cm.
• Baby corn 25 kg (10 kg per acre) per ha, spacing should be 60x20cm.
• Popcorn 12 kg per ha (5 kg per acre), spacing should be 60x20cm.
• Green cob 20 kg per ha (8 kg per acre), spacing should be 75x20cm.
Tips:
• Higher rainfall and more fertile soil can respond well to higher plant population densities.
• For most conditions, a 5 cm depth for dry planting is ideal.
• If you are planting in moist soil, the following maximum planting depths are 7 cm in heavy clay soil, 9 cm in both light clay soil and in heavy silt clay soil.
• Use a planting stick with two pointers at a 5 cm distance. One pointer should be 5 cm long for seeds, and another 7 cm long for fertilizer.
• Immediately after seeding and fertilizer application, cover the seed and fertilizer with fine soil.
• Planting maize in lines is important for uniform and optimal plant population, easy intercultural operations like weeding, fertilizer application, and pest-disease control, as well as for harvesting and a better yield.
