Time for Harvest

Week 19

The appearance of the plant may be misleading, particularly in the case of high-yielding composites and hybrids whose grains are dry while the stalk and leaves may be still green.
• The maize crop raised for grain is harvested when the grains are nearly dry and do not contain more than 20% moisture.
• Harvest your crop when 90% of the total cobs are dry.
• Remove ear heads or cobs from the standing crop.
• Harvested ear-heads cobs are dried in the sun. Harvest the whole plants and pile them, and remove the ear heads later.
• Removed grains from cobs by hand shelling or using a mechanical maize sheller.
• Use maize stalks as a cattle feed and fuel.The stover/cobs from which the grains are removed are also used as a fuel.
• No part of the maize plant is left unused.
• The fodder maize is harvested at 50% flowering stage, which produces maximum yield or dry matter with fairly nutritious forage.
