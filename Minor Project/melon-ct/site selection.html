Optimal conditions for watermelon

3 Weeks before seedling

• Watermelon is a warm-season crop and requires dry weather with abundant sunshine for quality fruit production.
• Temperature range of 24-27°C is considered optimal for the growth of the vines.
• Cool nights and warm days are ideal for the accumulation of sugars in the fruits.
• The seed germinates best when temperatures are higher than 20°C.
• High humidity at the time of vegetative growth renders the crop susceptible to various fungal diseases.
• Watermelons can be grown on well-drained sandy to sandy loam, medium-black soils rich in organic matter.
• Soil along the river beds is also good for the production of watermelons.
• A soil pH range of 6.0-7.0 is considered optimal.
